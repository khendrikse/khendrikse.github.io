People are tech's greatest asset. Especially when they are diverse and get the chance to thrive in their personal and professional lives. Finding a good work-life balance is difficult though, as this field is so good at sucking people in.

## Let's finish a day of work!

_Let's do a little thought experience:_

> _You close your laptop for the day (if you even do this at all). You’re scrolling through twitter or reddit for the latest tech, talks or drama. You are obviously missing out. Should you spend some time looking into that new framework update at after dinner? Or watch that talk everyone is excited about?_

> _You also have an insane list of articles piling up in your ‘to-read’ list. Oh... and remember that personal project you were so excited about two months ago? Maybe continue working on that? Then again, your portfolio website needs some freshening up... how about using that new thing people have been talking about? At least then you’ll be able to stay up-to-date with the latest tech._

> _If you’re real lucky you are a self-taught developer, and you might have a stack of books ('learning about algorithms', 'everything you missed out on when you were not studying CS') to get you up to speed with everything you obviously missed in education. Hopefully those will help combat that ever lurking fear that, even though you bring valuable knowledge from other jobs and life experiences, might you simply, not be good enough?_

Obviously, the above could be more nuanced, but there are enough professionals out there who might recognize some of these thoughts.

### Taking a turn for the best

Let’s forget about the pressure of feeling like you can’t keep up, or the complexities (and joy) of having a job that is also a hobby. Instead, let’s dive into things that might positively change to your life... It might even trickle down into your work! How wonderful that things that are good for you as a person can help you become more focussed, creative and productive.

## The pick n mix list

If any of these tips resonate with you, try it out! A small change can make a huge difference!
* [Move your body](#Move-your-body)


### Move your body

Moving your body to limit sedentary behaviour can be life saving! It can also do wonders for the way you feel about yourself. Not because of the possible impact it has on your physique, no! Because brains love those hormones it produces. Think of it as a free and healthy snack to get you through the day. Thankfully you do not need to be the sporty type to make this happen. There is a bunch of stuff you can do in small steps just to create habits.

- Take a quick 3 minute break every 30 minutes. Why would you ruin your focus? There are many health reasons but moving focus can also help you solve problems.

  **Extra hacks:**
  - Take a small stretch or walk around a little if this is possible for you.
  - Look outside the window.

- Try to take a 30 minute walk at least once a day.

  **Extra hacks:**

  - Bring along your kid or dog.
  - Try a different route every day
  - Call someone you like or have your meeting while walking
  - Listen to music or a podcast
  - Try a walking meditation
  - Get an umbrella for rainy days, or rain boots to enjoy splashing puddles.
- Pick up a sport that you could truly enjoy. Don’t be afraid to stop if it does not feel right, just try stuff out until something sticks. And nothing does? No problem! That is no big deal whatsoever.

Embrace and expand your hobbies
Coding is great, for many developers it is also a hobby. But there is so much more! Spark your imagination and go see what is out there!
Extra tips:

- What did you like as a kid?
- Ask friends, family or colleagues what they enjoy doing.
- Is there a fun (online) course that you can do?

Try out some stuff. Just don’t be too hard on yourself when learning a new thing. Most beginnings can be hard. Heard of wabi-sabi? No? Search it up!

Reflect
There is a chance that you are familiar with retrospectives. If not, no worries. Reflecting on your day, week or month can be quite good for your brain. This can be through writing down thoughts, feelings or things you like. But also through simply allowing yourself to process your thoughts without judging yourself.
Extra tips:

- use pen and paper to write down your thoughts.
- Get a buddy and meet up every now and then for a moment to discuss each other.

Find time for families, friends and new contacts
Spending time with people gives some individuals energy, but for some it can actually drain energy. While respecting this, finding ways to have social interactions, also when this might be a challenge, is good for your health! Small interactions can make a huge difference.

Extra tips:

- make an effort to visit/call friends or family. Ask people to talk about themselves and their history. It is fun to get to know more about people you like spending time with, and people simply love talking about themselves
- Play a game with people! Remote or in real life.
